Overview:
The entire faction has been reworked from the ground up, giving the Hiigarans a specific combat theme, new weapons, new ships, and new fighters. All of the issues with blueprints, markets selling things they shouldn't or not selling things they should, etc, should be fixed. All the ships have had their hardpoints tweaked, all the firing arcs better represent the placement and shape of the ships, a lot of hardpoints have been changed in size, and some new hardpoints were added on some ships. All the weapons have been tweaked for balance and to give them more defined roles. 

Battle Theme:
The Hiigaran Navy prefers a defensive posture, an ideal that dates back to their early spacefaring epoch and all the hardships that came with it. Their ships are both fast and tough, with many of their ships still being from the Great Exodus, but their offensive capabilities are lacking due to very poor flux management systems. Their armaments are both ballistic and hybrid weapons with a mix of missiles. Typically their weaponry is flux efficient and long ranged but low damage, which plays to both their strengths and weaknesses. They have unique drone systems that allow some ships to deploy specialty drones for point defense or support. 

Notes:

All the balance was done from a viewpoint of vanilla and a few mods such as Ship/Weapon Pack and DME. Their ships are fairly strong in the player's hands due to the enemy's AI often not being aggressive when needed, but it's not over the top. The weapons are in line with vanilla counterparts, lower flux useage with comparatively lower damage output for their OP cost. The top tier fighters are a bit on the strong side, but very high OP costs, expensive, and rather rare. Some of their ships are very rare or don't have blueprints, so keep a look out for ships you've never seen before, because you might not see them again for a while.

v3:

The way the game handles burst fire weapons is very strange and has to be taken on a weapon-by-weapon basis, making a dps calculator tool incorrect, which has rendered initial balance wonky. I decided to take the time to do another balance pass on weapons altogether.

Tweaked the cost of all weapons

Changed Pulsar Beams to be more of a cheap assault/anti-fighter weapon. No longer has charges, just a cooldown. No longer targets missiles, but prioritizes fighters over other ships. Compared to other hiigaran weapons they have worse efficiency and range but better DPS and armor-penetration. 
Heavy Pulsar Beam is now called Medium Pulsar Beam, it's OP cost has been reduced to 10, and it's geared towards being a cheap medium slot option.
Kinetic Drivers, Sandstorms, and vanilla Burst Lasers filled a much better PD role than Pulsar Beams, so shifting them towards a harder hitting assault role gives them a less saturated role.

Increased the overall DPS of all three sequenced pulsars, while keeping the flux efficiency the same
Increased the DPS of all kinetic burst cannons, slightly improved their accuracy, increased their OP costs by 1, keeping them in line with, but distinct from, vanilla autocannons and needlers
Reduced the DPS of the Rail Cannon

Improved the tracking effectiveness and damage of the Cor missile
Tweaked the visuals of all missiles

The Tviir missile is now a nearly unguided, short ranged rocket with high burst damage
